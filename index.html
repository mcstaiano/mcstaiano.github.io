About

What you should know about me?

Hello there! I'm Maria Carmen — researcher, language enthusiast, and passionate advocate for making machine translation and language technologies work in the moments that matter most. I believe in concrete actions and real-world impact: language tech should be accessible and reliable for everyone, especially in crisis and healthcare situations.

I am currently pursuing a PhD in Machine Translation and Language Technologies at the University of Macerata, Italy. My research focuses on evaluating MT and LLMs within crisis communication and high-stakes domains such as public health.

Profile

Machine Translation NLP Crisis Communication Healthcare Communication Translation Technologies
  • PhD Candidate in MT & Language Technologies — University of Macerata
  • Research focus: evaluating MT and LLMs in crisis communication and public health domains
  • Investigating how language technologies perform in high-stakes, real-world scenarios

Previously, I completed studies in languages and linguistics, building a strong foundation in translation theory and practice. I have experience working with multilingual content across a range of domains, and I am particularly interested in how computational approaches can support human communication in critical situations.

Trying to create a positive impact in the world

Outside academia, I am passionate about the social dimensions of language and technology. My research is driven by the belief that machine translation and language technologies should be accessible, reliable, and beneficial for everyone — especially in critical moments such as emergencies, crises, and healthcare situations.

I am committed to bridging the gap between cutting-edge research and real-world needs, and to making language technology work for those who need it most.
