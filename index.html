I am currently pursuing a PhD in Machine Translation and Language Technologies at the University of Macerata, Italy. My doctoral research focuses on evaluating Machine Translation (MT) and Large Language Models (LLMs) within crisis communication and other high-stakes domains, such as public health.

Profile

I'm a PhD Candidate at University of Macerata, Italy, working on Machine Translation and Language Technologies for crisis and healthcare communication.

Machine Translation NLP Crisis Communication Healthcare Communication Translation Technologies
  • PhD Candidate in Machine Translation & Language Technologies — University of Macerata
  • Research focus: evaluating MT and LLMs in crisis communication and public health high-stakes domains
  • Investigating how language technologies perform in real-world critical scenarios

Previously, I completed my studies in languages and linguistics, developing a strong foundation in translation theory and practice. I have experience working with multilingual content across a range of domains, and I am particularly interested in how computational approaches can support human communication in critical situations.

Community, Volunteering & Some Results

"Educating the mind without educating the heart is no education at all"

Trying to create a positive impact in the world

Outside academia, I am passionate about the social dimensions of language and technology. My research is driven by the belief that machine translation and language technologies should be accessible, reliable, and beneficial for everyone — especially in critical moments such as emergencies, crises, and healthcare situations.

I am committed to bridging the gap between cutting-edge research and real-world needs, and to making language technology work for those who need it most.

If you'd like to collaborate, discuss ideas, or exchange experiences — feel free to reach out!
